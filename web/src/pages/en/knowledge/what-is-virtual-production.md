**Virtual production** is a shooting method in which digital environments are rendered in real time on a large LED wall and captured directly in camera. Instead of filming against a green screen and adding the world later in post, the finished backdrop is already there on set — visible to the crew, the camera and the client.

## How does virtual production work?

Three building blocks work together:

- **LED wall (the “volume”):** A high-resolution wall displays the environment — a desert, a night skyline, a factory floor or an abstract brand world.
- **Camera tracking:** The camera's position is captured in real time. As the camera moves, the background shifts in a perspective-correct way — creating real spatial depth instead of a flat projection.
- **Real-time engine (Unreal Engine):** The environment is rendered live and can be adjusted in minutes: time of day, lighting, backdrop.

The result is **in-camera VFX**: the final look is created in the camera, not weeks later at the edit suite.

## How is it different from green screen?

With green screen the background is added afterwards — with the risk that reflections, light and focus don't match the scene. On the LED wall the light of the environment falls **for real** on people and objects: glasses, paint and metal reflect believably, and everyone sees the finished image immediately. No nasty surprises in post.

## Why does it matter for companies?

Virtual production is not just a cinema tool — it is especially powerful for B2B:

- **Five locations in one shoot day:** desert in the morning, skyline at midday, factory floor in the afternoon — without travel, permits or weather risk.
- **A budget you can plan:** travel and location costs disappear, and backdrop changes take minutes instead of travel days.
- **Reusable sets:** a virtual company set, built once, can be reused for every further production — the basis for content retainers.

Typical uses: brand and product films, recruiting videos, trade-fair films, keynotes and livestreams.

## Who is virtual production for?

Especially companies with complex products, recurring content needs or high demands on image quality — for example in medical technology, industry and the Mittelstand. If you previously had to travel far or book expensive large studios for such images, a regional LED stage is a predictable alternative.

> **In short:** virtual production brings Hollywood technology into a single room — with real reflections, a budget you can plan, and the freedom to step into any world on the same day.
